# Recontextualized equipment and items for the ruins

%%%%
# WEAPONS - Recontextualized as scavenged/improvised weapons
%%%%

%%%%
club

A crude improvised weapon - likely a piece of rebar or a chair leg.
Effective for bashing skulls, even if it's not elegant.
%%%%
whip

A length of chain or cable, useful for keeping threats at a distance
and disarming opponents. Raiders favor these for intimidation.
%%%%
dagger

A makeshift shiv or combat knife, standard issue for close encounters.
Quick, quiet, and deadly in skilled hands.
%%%%
short sword

A machete or large combat knife. Pre-war military issue or scavenged
from security forces. Reliable and versatile.
%%%%
long sword

A salvaged cavalry saber or extended combat blade. Excellent balance
and reach make it a prized weapon in the ruins.
%%%%
great sword

A massive scavenged blade - possibly from a piece of industrial
equipment. Requires considerable strength but devastates enemies.
%%%%
hand axe

A fire axe or hatchet, common in the ruins. Originally for breaching
doors and rescue work, now equally effective against skulls.
%%%%
war axe

A heavy industrial cutting tool or combat axe. Designed to cleave
through both obstacles and opponents with brutal efficiency.
%%%%
battleaxe

A two-handed monster of an axe, likely from a pre-war lumberjack or
military demolition unit. Few can wield it, fewer survive facing it.
%%%%
spear

A sharpened pole or improvised pike. Simple, effective, and keeping
your distance from wasteland creatures is always a good idea.
%%%%
mace

A length of pipe with one end weighted or studded. Crude but effective
for smashing armor and bones alike.
%%%%
flail

A chain with a weight attached - possibly a bike lock or industrial
flail. Devastating but hard to control.
%%%%
morningstar

A spiked ball on a chain or handle. Brutal and primitive, exactly
what you need in the wasteland.
%%%%

%%%%
# ARMOR - Recontextualized as scavenged protective gear
%%%%

%%%%
robe

Tattered pre-war clothing, offering minimal protection but maximum
mobility. Better than nothing, barely.
%%%%
leather armour

Makeshift armor crafted from thick leather, tire rubber, or layered
fabric. Standard scavenger protection against cuts and bites.
%%%%
ring mail

Improvised armor made from metal rings, bottle caps, or linked chains.
Some clever survivor's attempt at protection. Surprisingly effective.
%%%%
scale mail

Overlapping metal plates scavenged from vehicles or machinery. Heavy
but provides solid protection against blade and claw.
%%%%
chain mail

True chain armor, likely scavenged from a museum or security depot.
Rare and valuable, it's saved many lives in the ruins.
%%%%
plate armour

Heavy salvaged armor, possibly riot gear or industrial protective
equipment. You'll move slowly, but you'll survive what kills others.
%%%%
crystal plate armour

Pre-war experimental armor, possibly from a military research facility.
Lightweight ceramic composite that somehow survived the collapse.
The technology to make it is long lost.
%%%%
helmet

A salvaged hard hat, motorcycle helmet, or military headgear.
Your brain is your most valuable asset - protect it.
%%%%
buckler

A small makeshift shield - trash can lid, car door panel, or actual
tactical shield. Better to block than to bleed.
%%%%
shield

A proper shield crafted from scavenged metal. Possibly a riot shield
or expertly crafted from a road sign. Invaluable for survival.
%%%%
large shield

A massive piece of protective metal - car door, vault door section,
or military riot shield. Heavy, but you'll be glad you carried it.
%%%%

%%%%
# CONSUMABLES - Recontextualized as wasteland supplies
%%%%

%%%%
bread ration

Pre-packaged survival rations or salvaged preserved food. Tastes like
cardboard, but calories are calories in the wasteland.
%%%%
meat ration

Dried mutant creature meat or preserved pre-war jerky. Don't ask
where it came from, just be grateful for the protein.
%%%%
potion of curing

A salvaged medical stimpack or antibiotics. Basic wasteland medicine
that can treat minor wounds and common ailments.
%%%%
potion of heal wounds

Advanced pre-war medical technology - probably military grade. These
autoinjectors can seal wounds and stabilize injuries in seconds.
Incredibly valuable.
%%%%
potion of haste

Adrenaline shots or pre-war combat stimulants. Speeds up your reflexes
dramatically but leaves you exhausted afterward. Use wisely.
%%%%
potion of might

Military-grade enhancement drugs. Temporarily boosts your strength
and combat effectiveness. The old armies had some powerful chemistry.
%%%%
potion of resistance

Anti-radiation meds or broad-spectrum protective pharmaceutical
cocktail. Provides temporary resistance to environmental hazards.
%%%%
potion of magic

Mysterious pre-war nootropics or experimental cognitive enhancers.
Restores mental focus and energy. The mechanism is poorly understood.
%%%%
scroll of identify

A pre-war technical manual or diagnostic device. Helps you understand
the function of unknown salvaged technology.
%%%%
scroll of teleportation

An experimental emergency relocation device. Quantum technology that
somehow still works. Instantly transports you to a random location.
One-time use only.
%%%%
scroll of blinking

A short-range tactical teleporter. Military tech for quick battlefield
repositioning. Much more controlled than full teleportation.
%%%%
scroll of fog

A smoke grenade or chemical dispersal device. Creates obscuring clouds
for tactical advantage or emergency escape.
%%%%

%%%%
# MAGICAL ITEMS - Recontextualized as pre-war technology
%%%%

%%%%
wand

A salvaged energy weapon or experimental device. Limited power
remaining - each discharge brings it closer to being scrap.
%%%%
wand of flame

A pre-war incendiary weapon. Fires concentrated bursts of heat.
Effective but power cells are nearly depleted.
%%%%
wand of iceblast

Cryo-weapon from military research labs. Fires supercooled projectiles
that can freeze targets solid. Rare and powerful.
%%%%
wand of paralysis

Neural disruptor or taser weapon. Overloads nervous systems to
temporarily paralyze targets. Non-lethal but extremely effective.
%%%%
ring

A piece of pre-war personal technology, worn on the finger. Some still
function, providing passive benefits to the wearer. The old world's
craftsmanship was remarkable.
%%%%
amulet

A neck-worn device, possibly medical, military, or corporate executive
equipment. Advanced technology packaged in jewelry-like form.
%%%%
amulet of faith

A faction identification device. Wearing it shows your allegiance and
grants benefits from faction technology networks still operating.
%%%%
amulet of regeneration

Advanced nano-medical device. Accelerates natural healing by orders of
magnitude. Military officers and corporate executives had access to
such technology before the collapse.
%%%%
ring of protection

Personal shield generator or protective field emitter. The weak
energy field it generates can deflect attacks.
%%%%
ring of strength

Powered exoskeleton control node. Enhances the wearer's physical
strength through micro-assists.
%%%%

# BOOKS - Recontextualized as technical manuals
%%%%

%%%%
book

A salvaged technical manual, training guide, or knowledge database.
The information within could teach you forgotten skills.
%%%%
Young Poisoner's Handbook

A pre-war chemistry textbook or black market guide to toxins. Teaches
methods for creating and using poisons effectively.
%%%%
Book of the Tempests

Military weather modification research or atmospheric manipulation
technology. Somehow this knowledge translates to controlling storms.
%%%%
Book of Fire

Industrial thermodynamics or weapons research manual. Understanding
it grants control over fire and heat.
%%%%
Book of Ice

Cryogenics research or cold storage technology documentation. The
principles within allow manipulation of extreme cold.

# MISCELLANEOUS
%%%%

%%%%
gold

Pre-war currency, jewelry, or valuable metal. Still valuable for
trade and crafting. Some settlements mint their own coins now.
%%%%
rune of Zot

A security keycard or authorization device. Required to access
the most secure pre-war facilities. Whatever they were guarding
must have been important.
%%%%
Orb of Zot

The legendary core of the most secure pre-war facility. A powerful
energy source or doomsday device - no one knows for certain. Every
faction wants it. Recovering it could change the wasteland forever.

# FLAVOR TEXT ADDITIONS

%%%%
# General wasteland context
%%%%

%%%%
salvaged weapon

Scavenged from the ruins and maintained through ingenuity and
necessity. Every weapon in the wasteland has a history.
%%%%
pre-war technology

Relics from before the Great Collapse. The old world had capabilities
we can barely understand now, let alone replicate.
%%%%
makeshift equipment

Improvised gear assembled from whatever materials are available.
Wasteland survivors are nothing if not resourceful.
%%%%
military hardware

Salvaged from old military installations or dead soldiers. This
equipment was designed for the wars that ended civilization.
%%%%
corporate artifact

High-end technology that executives and security forces used before
the collapse. Rare and valuable.
%%%%
